老師：obligate /ˈɒblɪɡeɪt/ (BrE) ｜ /ˈɑːblɪɡeɪt/ (AmE) (verb); also adjectival use in technical contexts (obligate)

中文（繁體）
- 意思（動詞）：使有義務、強制、迫使（某人做某事）；在法律或合約上使負有責任或義務。
- 意思（形容詞，專業用法）：專性地需要（某種條件）的，例如生物學上常見的專性寄生（obligate parasite）、專性需氧（obligate aerobe）。
- 文法：動詞為及物（obligate somebody/something to do something）。當表示「覺得有義務」或「被迫做」時，常見過去分詞形容詞為 obligated/obliged（注意 obliged 也可表示「感激」）。
- 常見搭配：obligate someone to do sth、be legally/contractually obligated to、obligate funds、obligate to report。

English
- Meaning (verb): to compel or bind (someone) legally, morally, or by necessity; to make something necessary.  
- Meaning (adj., technical): (esp. in biology) requiring a specific condition (e.g., obligate anaerobe = requires absence of oxygen).
- Grammar: transitive verb: obligate sb/sth to do sth. Passive/adjectival forms: obligated / obliged (note: "obliged" can mean thankful).

例句 Examples（含中文翻譯）
1. The contract obligates the tenant to pay rent on the first of each month.  
   合約規定房客有義務在每月一號付租金。  
2. New regulations obligate companies to report their emissions every year.  
   新法規強制公司每年申報排放量。  
3. He felt obligated to attend the funeral even though he disagreed.  
   儘管不同意，他還是覺得有義務去參加葬禮。  
4. In biology, many obligate parasites can only reproduce inside a host cell.  
   在生物學上，許多專性寄生物只能在宿主細胞內繁殖。  
5. Obligate aerobes require oxygen to survive.  
   專性需氧生物需要氧氣才能存活。  
6. The government is legally obligated to protect citizens' rights.  
   政府在法律上有義務保護公民的權利。  
7. The department will obligate funds for the project as soon as the budget is approved.  
   預算一經核准，該部門就會撥款給該專案。  
8. Don't confuse "obligated" (compelled) with "obliged" in the phrase "I'm much obliged" (meaning "thank you").  
   不要把表示「被迫」的 obligated 和片語 "I'm much obliged"（表示「非常感謝」）搞混了。

同義詞 / 反義詞
- 同義詞（動詞）：compel, require, force, bind  
- 反義詞：free, release, exempt

使用建議
- 在法律、合約或正式書寫中常用 obligate（或 be obligated to）。  
- 口語中談到「幫個忙／感謝」時用 obliged（I'm obliged/I'm much obliged）比較自然；表示被迫時可用 obligated。  
- 在生物／醫學文獻中，obligate 作為形容詞非常常見（obligate parasite / obligate anaerobe）。
